This ceiling begs to be smashed

Glass may be transparent but we still know it’s there.

Which is exactly why we use “the glass ceiling” to describe the unspoken, unwritten and unacknowledged limitations imposed upon women and minorities in their professional careers.

4.8%
of S&P 500 CEOs are women.1
22.5%
of Fortune 500 board seats are filled by women.2
4.6%
of Fortune 500 board seats are filled by female minorities.

It’s time we answer the rhetoric with action.

Here’s how you can help smash through the glass ceiling

1

As women in positions of power, hire your own.

Once you’ve worked your way to the top, extend the opportunity to your female peers. As a female executive or director, you have the power to bring more women into senior management roles.

2

As minorities in an organization, be loud about your wins.

You bring a unique perspective, so talk about it and celebrate it—especially with your supervisors. Your company benefits from your world view—and those above you should be reminded.

3

As men with inherent advantage, actively challenge your personal biases.

Be conscious about giving your female colleagues the voice and recognition they deserve—for every male you sponsor within your organization, sponsor a female colleague as well.

Each time you acknowledge women and minorities who are accomplishing incredible things, the ceiling gets weaker.
